Course Objectives:
  • This is a 3-credit course aiming to provide the students with a major engineering design experience.
  • The student is required to function as a team member to design, construct, and test mobile robots.
  • When this course is completed the student should be able to:
    • gain knowledge about the historical development of robotics, mobile robot locomotion, sensing, and control.
    • learn the hardware architecture, interface, and programming language (interactive C or assembly) of Motorola 68HC11 microprocessor.
    • apply knowledge (circuits, control theory, etc), skills, and tools to design complex systems containing both hardware and software components.
    • improve the ability to design and conduct experiments, as well as analyze
      and interpret data.
    • improve the problem-solving ability, i.e., the ability to identify, formulate and solve engineering problems.
    • improve communication skill by means of written report, and oral presentation.
    • recognize the importance of team work.
Project Description:

Students are divided into 3-4 design teams to build miniature mobile robots. The robots should be able to detect, pick up cola cans, and put them in a designated area while avoiding the obstacles which scattered randomly within an 8X8 square feet boundary. The design teams are encouraged to construct different robot structures, design different locomotion methods, and use different sensors. The competition between the design teams is judged by the performance of their robots, i.e., the team whose robot completes the task in shortest time is the champion.

LEGO MINDSTORMS set will be used for students to build the robot bodies. The advantages of using MINDSTORMS set are:

  • MINDSTORMS sets include gears, axles, worm racks, wheels and other
    components. It is easy for students to build complicated robot structures with
    grippers and efficient locomotion mechanism.
  • Students can concentrate on the control, sensing, and programming aspects of the
    design, rather than the mechanical aspect.
Handyboard with 68HC11 micro-controller will be used as the "brain" of the robots. Students are required to write C program code to integrate the sensor, control and actuator into the robot system.

Excellent students are encouraged to form an elite team to participate the
Micro-mouse Design Competition representing the IEEE of CCNY.

Each design team is required to write an in depth engineering report and to make oral presentations to the faculty
