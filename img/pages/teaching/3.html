Robotics and Embedded Systems Design Track
Course Description:
EE598.66 and EE598.67 are a sequence of two semester capstone design courses.
The students are required to design and implement a solution to an engineering project.
The course consists of two components --- general lecture (1.5 class hours/week)
and design activities (3 design hours/week).  Topics of general lecture include
introduction to engineering design, identification of a problem, background research, social,
environmental, ethical and economic considerations, intellectual property and patents
and proposal writing, including methods of engineering analysis and theoretical modeling.
A detailed concept and design proposal shall be completed during the first semester and
the implementation phase may also begin. A functional physical prototype or computer
model shall be completed and tested in the second semester. For the second semester,
students are required to write a midterm progress report and an in depth engineering final
report. They are also required to make an oral final presentation and demonstration to
the faculty.

Project Topics:

Senior students in Electrical Engineering program and Computer Engineering program
who meet the pre-requisite requirement can select design projects from the robotics and
embedded systems design  track or come up with his/her own design projects.
Please check the Design Studio for detailed description of the project topics,
available resource, and special requirements.
